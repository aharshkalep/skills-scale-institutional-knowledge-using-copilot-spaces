# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing specialists ensure product quality through validation, test planning, and defect management. They work closely with developers and product teams to verify acceptance criteria and maintain quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Create and execute test plans (functional, regression, integration)
- Validate features against acceptance criteria
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Collaborate on test automation strategies
- Provide quality feedback during planning and reviews

### Goals
- Ensure high product quality and user satisfaction
- Catch issues early in the development cycle
- Improve test coverage and automation

### Typical Communication
- Test case reviews with developers
- Bug triage meetings
- Sprint planning and retrospectives
- QA status in daily standups

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs and ensure the product is intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs. They conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and collaborate with product and engineering teams.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Ensure accessibility and design system compliance
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-friendly experiences
- Reduce user friction and support tickets
- Maintain design consistency across products

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with product and engineering
- User research findings and recommendations
- Prototype walkthroughs
- Design handoff documentation

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain documentation that helps users, developers, and stakeholders understand and use the product effectively. They translate complex technical concepts into clear, accessible content.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user guides, API documentation, and release notes
- Collaborate with developers to understand technical features
- Create onboarding materials and tutorials
- Ensure documentation accuracy and consistency
- Manage documentation feedback and updates

### Goals
- Enable users to be successful with minimal support
- Reduce time-to-understanding for new features
- Maintain comprehensive, up-to-date documentation

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with engineering and product
- Content planning meetings
- Feedback sessions with support and users
- Release notes coordination

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and automation that enable reliable, efficient software delivery. They bridge development and operations to improve deployment velocity and system reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure, monitoring, and alerting
- Implement security best practices and compliance
- Automate deployment and operational tasks
- Support incident response and system reliability

### Goals
- Minimize deployment time and risk
- Ensure high availability and performance
- Enable developer productivity through automation

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure planning with engineering leads
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Security and compliance reviews
- Deployment coordination

---

## Customer Support Specialist

### Role Summary
Customer Support Specialists serve as the voice of the customer, providing assistance, gathering feedback, and ensuring customer success. They collaborate with product and engineering teams to resolve issues and improve the product.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to customer inquiries and support tickets
- Troubleshoot issues and escalate bugs to engineering
- Document common issues and solutions
- Provide customer feedback to product teams
- Create and maintain support knowledge base

### Goals
- Deliver excellent customer experience
- Reduce resolution time and ticket volume
- Identify product improvement opportunities

### Typical Communication
- Daily ticket triage and customer interactions
- Weekly sync with product and engineering on trends
- Bug reports and feature requests
- Knowledge base updates

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions by gathering requirements, analyzing data, and ensuring alignment between stakeholders and delivery teams. They help define success metrics and validate outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze data to inform decisions and measure outcomes
- Create process flows and business cases
- Facilitate requirement workshops with stakeholders
- Validate solutions meet business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure solutions deliver measurable business value
- Improve alignment between business and technical teams
- Enable data-driven decision making

### Typical Communication
- Requirement gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Data analysis presentations
- Process mapping workshops
- Business case reviews with leadership

## Rationale for Role Definitions

This document defines all team roles to address several key needs. The recent expansion to include UX Designer, Technical Writer, DevOps Engineer, Customer Support Specialist, and Business Analyst roles further enhances this foundation.

### Why These Updates Are Needed

**1. Clarity and Completeness**
Modern software projects involve diverse specialists beyond core development and product management. Documenting these roles prevents confusion about responsibilities, reduces duplication of effort, and ensures all team members understand how they contribute to project success.

**2. Onboarding Efficiency**
New team members need to quickly understand who does what and how to collaborate effectively. Clear role definitions accelerate onboarding by providing a reference for typical responsibilities, goals, and communication patterns.

**3. Accountability and Ownership**
Explicitly defining roles like DevOps Engineer and Technical Writer ensures critical activities (infrastructure, documentation) have clear owners. This prevents these responsibilities from being overlooked or falling between the cracks.

**4. Cross-functional Collaboration**
Projects succeed when teams understand how different roles interact. Including UX Designer and Business Analyst roles highlights the importance of user-centered design and business alignment throughout the project lifecycle.

**5. Team Velocity and Quality**
Having dedicated specialists (QA/Testing, Technical Writer, Customer Support) enables the team to move faster while maintaining quality. Developers can focus on implementation while other experts handle specialized tasks like test strategy, documentation, and customer feedback.

**6. Stakeholder Engagement**
Customer Support Specialists and Business Analysts play crucial roles in gathering feedback and ensuring solutions meet real needs. Documenting these roles emphasizes the importance of continuous stakeholder engagement.
